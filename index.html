My Mission

I love building things that people love to use. Ever since I was a teenager, I knew that any software I built would have the foremost purpose of making people's lives better. As a kid, that was making my friends an awesome new Counter Strike server on Linux, or running a phpBB roleplaying forum with hundreds of active members. As an adult, it's been contributing to the decentralized Web3 ecosystem that I believe will change the internet forever, and give people more control over their digital/real lives. My work experience has been with major companies that have given me an eye-opening view of what goes into building resilient infrastructure, elegant software, and top-notch teams. I'm thankful for the opportunities I've had thus far and everything I've learned about software architecture, design patterns, modern CI/CD deployment, algorithmic thinking, and large organization coordination. With those lessons, I am excited to start help building the future of software.

Back-End

Python: I mainly use Python for smaller projects due to its fast-prototyping and expansive library. Java: First used in school, I'm quite familiar with the JVM ecosystem, and have been looking into using Kotlin. Go: Picked up in my internship, and now enjoy using it in my personal projects due to its speed, portability, and distributed networking-focused services.

Tech

Flask: My go-to for any webapp that uses a Python backend; I enjoy the Jinja2 templating language and the easy routing that is afforded by Flask. Docker: First exposed during my time at AWS, and now I try to deploy most of my individual ventures using it. MongoDB: I've been using MongoDB during my internship and personal projects due to its simplicity and querying system.

Tools

Linux: Started using Linux when I was 13, out of wanting to run cloud gaming servers, and now it's my daily driver as I run Manjaro and Arch. JetBrains: I mainly use Goland, Pycharm, IntelliJ IDEA, Clion etc. as my primary IDEs, but will sometimes use Vim for smaller work. Git: Very familiar with using Git/GitHub in my personal and professional life.

Featured Projects

Bogged.me

I led a duo of my friend and I during GMU's PatriotHacks 2021 winter hackathon. We ended up winning with our Flask and MongoDB based paper trading app for cryptocurrency. I was able to take 2 weeks to learn how to build a full-fledged web application, use Bootstrap and Javascript for the first time, and implement several different APIs to get real-time price tracking and transaction information for our app. It was a great experience, and I ended up launching our application on Heroku available today (however it is still very much a work-in-progress due to being a Hackathon project).
